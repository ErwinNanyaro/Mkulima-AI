Our Mission & Vision

Founder Story

Erwin Nanyaro

Hailing from Tanzania, Erwin Nanyaro is an agricultural technologist with a passion for empowering smallholder farmers through appropriate technology. His journey into agricultural innovation began with the transformative CLIFT project research in 2024, a collaborative initiative between Mzumbe University and the University of Copenhagen.

While conducting surveys across five Tanzanian regions (Morogoro, Dodoma, Iringa, Njombe, and Mbeya), Erwin witnessed firsthand the technological gaps limiting smallholder farmers. The lack of accessible, localized digital tools for these farmers - who form the backbone of Tanzania's agriculture - inspired the creation of Mkulima AI.

Recognizing that 80% of Tanzania's farmers are smallholders often excluded from digital solutions, Erwin dedicated himself to developing an AI platform that works offline, in Swahili, and addresses real farmer needs identified through extensive field research.

Mkulima AI represents the convergence of Erwin's technical skills with his deep understanding of rural farming challenges - creating a tool that bridges the digital divide while respecting traditional farming knowledge.

Research & Development

Mkulima AI is built on extensive field research conducted during the CLIFT project in May 2024. We visited 10 villages across 5 regions, collecting data directly from smallholder farmers about their challenges, needs, and technological access.

Our findings revealed:

  • 92% of farmers had smartphones but only 28% used agricultural apps
  • Internet connectivity was unreliable in 87% of farming areas
  • Language barriers prevented adoption of existing solutions
  • Farmers wanted tools that combined traditional knowledge with modern science

This research directly informed Mkulima AI's development priorities: offline functionality, Swahili interface, and integration of local farming wisdom.
