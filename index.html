Founder Story

Erwin Nanyaro

Hailing from Tanzania, Erwin Nanyaro is a project scholar and AI self taught technologist with a passion for empowering smallholder farmers through appropriate technology. His journey into agricultural innovation began with the transformative CLIFT project research in 2024, a collaborative initiative between Mzumbe University and the University of Copenhagen.

While conducting surveys across two Tanzanian regions (Morogoro, and Arusha), Erwin witnessed firsthand the technological gaps limiting smallholder farmers. The lack of accessible, localized digital tools for these farmers - who form the backbone of Tanzania's agriculture - inspired the creation of Mkulima AI.

Recognizing that 80% of Tanzania's farmers are smallholders often excluded from digital solutions, Erwin dedicated himself to developing an AI platform that works offline, in Swahili, and addresses real farmer needs identified through extensive field research.

Mkulima AI represents the convergence of Erwin's technical skills with his deep understanding of rural farming challenges - creating a tool that bridges the digital divide while respecting traditional farming knowledge.

Bridging the Gap: Climate Action Meets Sustainable Agriculture

In the heart of the Uluguru Mountains, I engaged with local leaders and strawberry farmers, witnessing firsthand the challenges in balancing agricultural productivity and water resource sustainability.

At Mkulima AI, we integrate AI-driven solutions to revolutionize water management and organic farming. Our initiative uses AI-powered hydrological sensors and predictive analytics to ensure efficient irrigation and improve soil health.

We're calling on Tanzania's leading institutions - Sokoine University, Mzumbe University, and NM-AIST - to join us in pushing the boundaries of innovation in sustainable farming. Collaboration with global partners like University of Copenhagen and WWF can help integrate climate-smart practices for rural resilience.

This is just the beginning of our vision where people and nature thrive together through AI-powered, climate-resilient agricultural systems.

The AI Revolution by Tanzanians, for Tanzanians

After teaching myself to code using generative AI, I built an app being tested in Turo, Kisosa, Morning Sight, Mlali, and Mgeta villages.

Unlike European houseplant apps, Mkulima AI is built in Swahili, with images of African farmers, and advice based on local solutions - not expensive imported inputs. It works offline for areas with poor coverage.

New features include weather alerts with farming advice and a profitability calculator using numbers farmers can easily collect. After the rainy season, we'll gather farmer feedback to improve the app.

This is the beginning of an AI revolution led by African youth - building tools to liberate ourselves from structures that hold us back. The dream is for Tanzanians to lead in developing solutions for our unique challenges.

From Code to Community Impact

In Maekani near Mzumbe, I met Eliah - a young farmer battling crop losses for 1.5 years, misled by expensive chemicals that didn't cure his plants' unknown diseases.

With one photo, Mkulima AI diagnosed Bell Pepper Early Blight and provided affordable, environmentally conscious solutions. His disbelief turned to hope as he finally understood what harmed his crops and how to fix it sustainably.

This moment validated why I built this app - to restore agency to farmers excluded from tech innovation. As climate change increases disease outbreaks, our solutions must be accessible, practical, and just.

Mkulima AI proves technology can be a tool of liberation when designed by and for the people it serves.

Hope in Action: One Farmer's Transformation

Returning to Eliah's farm weeks after introducing Mkulima AI revealed a remarkable transformation. His once-devastated pepper plants were recovering after implementing the app's recommendations:

  • Removing infected leaves to prevent spread
  • Applying neem oil as natural pesticide
  • Watering at the base to avoid leaf wetness
  • Mulching to prevent soil splash

Eliah's testimonial: "After removing the infected plants, truly I did not see that challenge anymore. The difference is huge... it helped me identify the disease and solve it... and that increased my harvests."

This tangible impact demonstrates how accessible AI can restore dignity and hope to smallholder farmers when rooted in local knowledge.

Real Impact in Maekani: From Struggle to Success

Last week, I met Eliah, a smallholder farmer from Maekani who had struggled for over a year with persistent pepper and tomato diseases. Despite trying countless chemical treatments, his crops continued to fail, threatening his family's livelihood.

Before: Diseased pepper plants

After: Healthy plants 3 weeks later

When Eliah tried Mkulima AI, the app quickly diagnosed Pepper Early Blight. With understandable hesitation but growing resolve, he implemented our recommendations:

  • Immediate removal of infected plants to prevent spread
  • Application of neem oil as organic pesticide
  • Adjusted watering techniques to avoid leaf wetness
  • Proper mulching to prevent soil splash contamination

"After removing the infected plants, truly I did not see that challenge anymore. The difference is huge... it helped me identify the disease and solve it... and that increased my harvests."

Eliah Zephania, Farmer

We documented every step of this transformation - from initial diagnosis through to full recovery. This case demonstrates how accessible AI technology can:

  • Provide accurate diagnoses without expensive lab tests
  • Offer affordable, sustainable solutions
  • Restore agency to smallholder farmers
  • Create measurable impact on food security

This is not just about AI technology - it's about restoring power to the people who feed our nations. Eliah's story represents thousands of smallholder farmers across Tanzania who just need the right tools to thrive.
